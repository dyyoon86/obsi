# gstack office-hours notes

Captured from reviewing `https://github.com/garrytan/gstack` during a SaaS ideation session.

## What gstack is

GStack is not a SaaS template generator. It is a Claude Code skill suite that turns AI into a product/engineering workflow: office hours, CEO review, engineering review, design review, QA, review, ship, and deployment-style processes.

## Relevant skills from the repo

- `/office-hours`: YC-style product diagnostic before code. Best fit for early SaaS idea validation.
- `/plan-ceo-review`: founder/strategy review. Challenges premise, scope, ambition, and wedge.
- `/plan-eng-review`: engineering plan review after the product direction is chosen.
- `/autoplan`: automatic review pipeline across multiple review skills.
- `/qa`, `/review`, `/ship`, `/land-and-deploy`: later-stage implementation and release discipline.

## Office-hours principles worth reusing

- Specificity is the only currency.
- Interest is not demand.
- The user's current workaround is the real competitor.
- Watch users; do not just demo to them.
- Narrow wedge beats platform vision early.
- End with a concrete assignment.

## Six forcing questions

1. Demand reality: strongest evidence someone wants this enough to be upset if it disappeared.
2. Status quo: what users do now, even badly, and what it costs them.
3. Desperate specificity: name the actual human/role who needs it most.
4. Narrowest wedge: smallest version someone would pay for this week.
5. Observation: what surprised you when watching someone use/do the workflow.
6. Future-fit: whether the product becomes more or less essential as the world changes.

## How to apply for this user

The user clarified they are not merely asking for content angles from a video/Threads example. They want to build a SaaS like the example. When this happens:

1. Acknowledge the pivot: “아, 콘텐츠가 아니라 너도 이런 SaaS를 만들고 싶은 거구나.”
2. Stop generating post scripts unless asked.
3. Switch to startup validation.
4. Start from their likely unfair advantage: AI news/content workflow, YouTube Shorts, Threads, Instagram, Notion DB.
5. But force proof that other people have the same pain and will pay.

## Suggested first assignment

For the AI-news-to-multiformat SaaS idea:

- Identify 10 specific target users who create AI/tech content.
- DM or post a validation prompt offering to manually turn one link into Shorts + Threads + Instagram assets.
- Count replies, links sent, and willingness to pay.
- Build only after getting concrete pull, not likes.
