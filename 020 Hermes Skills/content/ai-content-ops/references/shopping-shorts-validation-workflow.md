# Shopping Shorts / Affiliate Shorts Validation Workflow

Use when the user sends Korean shopping-shorts / side-income YouTube links or wants to test whether the approach can make money.

## Core framing

Treat these videos as **content-strategy and validation material**, not as guaranteed business advice. The user is interested in testing the model with Hermes support.

Default stance:

- Do not encourage blind copying of revenue claims.
- Separate what is reusable as a content/production formula from what is risky or exaggerated.
- Frame the first run as a **2-week validation experiment**, not a promise of monthly income.
- Emphasize measurable signals: views, profile visits, link clicks, comments/DMs asking where to buy, and actual affiliate revenue.

## Common pattern found in May 2026 Korean videos

Observed across shopping-shorts/growth videos:

1. Strong title formula: expensive course not needed + zero followers/beginners + high income + free full process.
2. Hook: show income proof or growth proof immediately.
3. Lower entry barriers: face-less, zero followers, no capital, beginners/50s/70s can do it.
4. Demonstrate screens: GPT, Coupang Partners, Inpock/Link-in-bio, CapCut, Typecast/TTS, Instagram/TikTok/YouTube Studio.
5. Use a free-value-to-lead-magnet funnel: free ebook, free live lecture, comment keyword, open chat room.
6. Revenue framing often combines affiliate clicks with YouTube Shorts AdSense or platform reach.

## Reality-check model

When asked “what is the chance I can earn from this?”, answer in ranges and explain the funnel.

Example rough probability framing:

- Copying videos exactly: low odds of meaningful income; maybe 10–20% chance of tiny revenue.
- 1–2 months with 60–100 uploads and iterative testing: roughly 20–35% chance of small side income (e.g. 100k–500k KRW/month), depending on execution.
- 100만+ KRW/month requires category focus, testing, and differentiation; not just downloading/reposting.
- 300만+ KRW/month is possible but low-probability for beginners; present as a top-end outcome, not expectation.

Explain the affiliate funnel:

`views → profile visit/comment/DM → link click → Coupang visit → 24h purchase → commission`

For Coupang-style 3% commission, show the order/click burden when useful. Example: to make 3,000,000 KRW/month at 35,000 KRW AOV and 3% commission requires about 2,857 orders. At 3–5% purchase conversion, that means roughly 57k–95k qualified clicks/month.

## Recommended 2-week experiment

Default recommendation for the user:

- Category: start with `살림템/생활꿀템`, with a narrower first angle such as kitchen/cleaning/storage.
- Target: 40–60대 여성 / household problem-solving audience.
- Output: 3 shorts/day for 14 days = 42 uploads.
- Cross-post: Instagram Reels first, then TikTok and YouTube Shorts.
- Goal: validate demand and click behavior, not immediate large income.

Continue if 2+ of these occur:

- Total views ≥ 100k.
- Link clicks ≥ 300.
- Any Coupang revenue occurs.
- Comments/DMs ask where to buy.
- At least one video exceeds 10k views.
- A repeated product category or hook pattern emerges.

Stop/pivot if after 42 uploads:

- Total views < 20k.
- Link clicks < 50.
- No comments/DMs indicating purchase intent.
- No product/hook pattern emerges.

## Product selection heuristics

Prefer products where the benefit is visually obvious in 1–3 seconds:

- Kitchen: vegetable chopper, oil splatter guard, sink drain filter, fridge organizer, pan/lid rack.
- Cleaning: mini handheld vacuum, bathroom stain brush, grease cleaner, lint remover.
- Laundry/storage: measuring detergent dispenser, gap storage rack, rice/grain container.
- Utility: automatic soap dispenser, wireless LED sensor light.

Avoid starting with:

- Big brand products (harder to source/position safely).
- Expensive electronics with high trust burden.
- Products where benefit cannot be shown visually.
- Claims requiring medical/health proof.

## 15-second script formula

Use short Korean spoken lines with minimal gaps:

1. 0–2s: Problem hook.
2. 2–5s: Product appears and core function is visible.
3. 5–10s: Show 1–2 concrete use cases.
4. 10–13s: State everyday benefit.
5. 13–15s: CTA.

Template:

```text
[문제] 때문에 매번 불편하셨죠?
이건 [제품명/기능]이라 [핵심 기능]을 바로 할 수 있어요.
[상황1], [상황2] 때 특히 편하고,
[생활 이득]까지 줄어듭니다.
제품 정보는 댓글에 “[키워드]” 남겨주세요.
```

## Production notes

- First 1–2 seconds must show either the pain point or product result.
- Keep sentences short; remove TTS gaps and dead air.
- One sentence should usually be 1.5–2.5 seconds.
- Captions must be readable; avoid long subtitle blocks.
- CTA must be explicit: comment keyword, profile link number, or DM automation.
- Include affiliate disclosure: `쿠팡 파트너스 활동의 일환으로 일정액의 수수료를 제공받습니다.`

## Safety and platform risk

Shopping-shorts videos often recommend downloading Douyin/TikTok/Taobao clips and covering Chinese subtitles. Treat this as risky.

Warn about:

- Copyright claims.
- Reused/duplicate content detection.
- Low originality / monetization rejection.
- Account trust issues.
- Coupang Partners disclosure/policy violations.

Safer adaptations:

- Use direct product footage where possible.
- Buy/test some products if a pattern works.
- Add original voiceover, structure, comparison, and clear consumer-use framing.
- Avoid copying the exact same clips, script, captions, and CTA from competitors.
- Do not overstate product claims; avoid medical, safety, or guaranteed-result claims.

## How Hermes should help

For each product candidate, produce a compact package:

- Product angle
- 1–2 second hook
- 15-second Korean script
- CTA keyword
- Caption text
- Hashtags
- Risk notes
- What metric to watch after posting

After the user posts, ask for or analyze results:

- Views
- Retention if available
- Profile visits
- Link clicks
- Comments/DMs
- Coupang revenue

Then update the next batch: more of the working category/hook, less of the weak ones.
